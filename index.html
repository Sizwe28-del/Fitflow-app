    Frequently Asked Questions

    How often should I work out each week?

    For beginners, 3-4 days per week is optimal. Intermediate and advanced exercisers can train 4-6 days per week. Always include at least 1-2 rest days to allow your body to recover.

    How long before I see results?

    Most people begin to feel differences in energy and strength within 2 weeks. Visible physical changes typically appear within 4-8 weeks, depending on your consistency, intensity, nutrition, and individual factors.

    Should I do cardio or weight training first?

    It depends on your primary goal. If building strength is your main focus, do weight training first when your energy is highest. If cardiovascular fitness or weight loss is your priority, you might prefer cardio first. You can also split them into separate sessions entirely.

    How can I reduce belly fat specifically?

    You cannot spot-reduce fat from specific areas. The best approach is a combination of caloric deficit through nutrition, regular cardio exercise, and full-body strength training. Core-specific exercises will strengthen the muscles but won't directly burn the fat covering them.

    What should I eat before and after workouts?

    Before workouts, aim for easily digestible carbs and some protein 1-3 hours prior (e.g., banana and yogurt, toast with egg). After workouts, consume protein and carbs within 45 minutes to support recovery (e.g., protein shake with fruit, chicken and rice).
